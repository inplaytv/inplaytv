## What is Strokes Gained?

**Strokes Gained (SG)** is the most advanced golf statistic that answers one simple question: *"How much better or worse is this player compared to the average PGA Tour player?"*

Think of it like this:
- **0.0 = Average PGA Tour performance** (the baseline)
- **Positive numbers (+) = Better than average** (gaining strokes on the field)
- **Negative numbers (-) = Worse than average** (losing strokes to the field)

|----------|---------------|--------------|

| **+1.5 to +2.5** | Excellent | Top 30 players |
| **+0.5 to +1.5** | Very good | Top 50-75 players |
| **-0.5 to +0.5** | Average | Middle of the pack (75-125 rank) |
| **-1.0 to -0.5** | Below average | Struggling to maintain card |
| **-1.5 or lower** | Poor | Bottom tier players |

### Real-World Example

If a player has **SG Total: +1.2**, it means:
- Over 4 rounds (72 holes), they would beat the average PGA Tour player by **4.8 strokes**
- That's almost 5 shots better than half the field!
- This player would likely finish in the top 20-30

## ğŸ“š Why This Matters

Traditional stats like scoring average are misleading because:
- Easy courses = lower scores (doesn't mean better play)
- Hard courses = higher scores (doesn't mean worse play)
- Doesn't account for course difficulty or field strength

**Strokes Gained solves this** by comparing every shot to PGA Tour average in that exact situation:
- Same distance
- Same lie (fairway, rough, bunker)
- Same conditions
- Adjusted for course difficulty

It's the fairest way to measure golf performance! ğŸ†
